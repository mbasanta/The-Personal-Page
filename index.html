Who: My name is Matthew Basanta. I spend 13 years in the U.S. Air Force, six of those years active duty and seven in the KY Air National Guard. I'm completely out of the military now and work in an office environment, the greatest threat I face is old coffee or the occasional paper cut. I spend much of my spare time cycling the backroads of central Kentucky and imagining I'm racing Wiggo up the Alpe d'Huez.

What: I'm going to ride Undefeated EOD Bike Ride. This nearly 200 mile ride is an annual event to benefit the Wounded EOD Warrior Foundation.

When: 12-13 October 2013

Where: The route starts at the EOD Memorial near Niceville, FL, and travels to Panama City Beach. The following day we'll return to the EOD Memorial.

Why: I'm raising money for the Wounded EOD Warrior Foundation. This organization supports wounded EOD Warriors and their families. To learn more about the 7,000 EOD Operators and what they do, check out this page for a quick overview.

How: I'm collecting donations for this ride. I want to raise $1000 for this event. The Wounded EOD Warrior Foundation is a 501(c)3 charitable organization so your donation is tax deductible. There are three ways to donate. You can go to the official donation page and donate online. When you donate on this page, $1 plus 6.5% is deducted as a service fee, if you would rather your entire donation go to the charity you can send me a check. You can also use Paypal, they also deduct a service fee, but it is less than than the official page.
